Forest Loss & Degradation

Forest degradation refers to the decline in the quality and health of forest ecosystems, often resulting from human activities. It encompasses a range of negative impacts on forests, such as deforestation, logging, unsustainable agriculture, mining, and infrastructure development. Forest degradation also impacts local communities that rely on forests for their livelihoods and cultural practices. Addressing forest degradation requires concerted efforts in sustainable land management, reforestation, and responsible resource extraction practices to ensure the long-term conservation and regeneration of our precious forest ecosystems.

Above Ground Biomass

Above-ground biomass refers to the total mass of living vegetation and organic matter present above the soil surface in a given area, such as trees, shrubs, and herbaceous plants. Above-ground biomass is an important measure of the carbon stored in terrestrial ecosystems, as plants absorb carbon dioxide from the atmosphere through photosynthesis and store it in their biomass. It serves as a crucial component of the global carbon cycle and plays a significant role in mitigating climate change by acting as a carbon sink.
