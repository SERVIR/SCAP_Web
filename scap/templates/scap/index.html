With S-CAP you can:

Monitor progress toward deforestation targets

Parties implementing REDD+ activities have set deforestation target’s within their region’s Forest Reference Emission Level Submissions. Through this tool, users can monitor progress towards these targets and determine the impact local decision making have on deforestation trends.

Monitor progress towards Net Zero deforestation

As committed through the UNFCCC, countries pledged their part to secure global net zero by mid-century. Users can track both deforestation and afforestation trends using various land cover change datasets to monitor the regions progress of net zero.

See emission estimations

USAID missions have a responsibility to track greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions as part of monitoring environmental dynamics in their respective regions. Users can estimate GHG emissions generated using various dataset combinations to better understand local trends.

Primary Data

The S-CAP methodology is based on those established by the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) and includes data layers describing the following:

Forest Cover & Forest Cover Loss

To characterize the carbon stocks contained within a region’s forest, one must understand an area’s historic and current forest coverage. Taking the difference in forest coverage between years provides the first half of variables needed to estimate emissions: activity data/area of deforestation.

Forest Above Ground Biomass

Different carbon pools (living biomass, litter and debris, soil) serve various purposes in terms of measuring and monitoring an area’s carbon stock. For estimating carbon emissions from deforestation, the S-CAP methodology focuses on the change in aboveground biomass (AGB) as it makes up the majority of a tree’s biomass in tropical forests.

Above Ground Biomass

Global

  • Avitable: 1km spatial resolution, Date 2016: harmonization of the pan-tropical datasets published by Saatchi et al. (2011) and Baccini et al. (2012)
    Citation: Avitabile, V, Herold, M, Heuvelink, GBM et al. (30 more authors) (2016) An integrated pan-tropical biomass map using multiple reference datasets. Global Change Biology, 22 (4). pp. 1406-1420. ISSN 1354-1013 https://doi.org/10.1111/gcb.13139 
  • Saatchi: 1km spatial resolution, Date 2011: Generated using ground measurements and GLAS (Geoscience Laser Altimeter System) lidar observations

  • GeoCarbon 2020: 1km spatial resolution, Date: 2020: Global map of aboveground biomass in forest, generated by combining and harmonizing the Avitabile pan-tropical biomass map with the boreal forest biomass map by Santoro et al. (2015).
    Citation: Santoro, M., Beaudoin, A., Beer, C., Cartus, O., Fransson, J.E.S., Hall, R.J., Pathe, C., Schmullius, C., Schepaschenko, D., Shvidenko, A., Thurner, M. and Wegmüller, U., 2015.   • GEDI: 1km spatial resolution, Date: 2019-04-18-2021-08-04: 
    Citation: Dubayah, R.O., J. Armston, S.P. Healey, Z. Yang, P.L. Patterson, S. Saarela, G. Stahl, L. Duncanson, and J.R. Kellner. 2022. GEDI L4B Gridded Aboveground Biomass Density, Version 2. ORNL DAAC, Oak Ridge, Tennessee, USA. https://doi.org/10.3334/ORNLDAAC/2017

Regional

  • ICIMOD 2015:  5km spatial resolution, Date 2015: From the field well distributed circular (750m2 and 500m2), 1124 field plots (0.001% representation of forest cover) measured which were used for estimation AGB (ton/ha) using Sharma et al. (1990) proposed equations for all tree species of Nepal. A satisfactory linear relationship (AGB = 8.7018*Hmax-101.24, R2=0.67, RMSE=7.2 ton/ha) achieved between maximum canopy height (Hmax) and AGB (ton/ha).
    Citation: ICIMOD. (2015). Above ground biomass (AGB) data in Nepal [Data set]. ICIMOD. https://doi.org/10.26066/rds.23172
