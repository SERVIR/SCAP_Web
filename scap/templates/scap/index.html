With S-CAP you can:

Monitor deforestation

REDD+ implementers have set deforestation reduction targets through their region’s Forest Reference Emission Level Submissions. With S-CAP, users can monitor progress towards deforestation reduction targets to determine the impact of local decision-making on deforestation trends.

Monitor progress towards net-zero deforestation

As part of the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC), countries pledged to contribute to global net zero deforestation by 2050. Users can track their region’s progress towards this goal using various land cover change datasets.

Estimate emissions

USAID missions have a responsibility to track greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions in their respective regions. Users can combine datasets to estimate GHG emissions on local or regional scales.

Primary Data

The S-CAP methodology is based on those established by the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) and includes data layers describing the following:

Forest Cover & Forest Cover Loss

Historic and current forest cover data are used to measure a region’s forest carbon stocks. Changes in forest cover over time help to determine areas of deforestation, a key variable in estimating carbon emissions.

Forest Above Ground Biomass

Different carbon pools (living biomass, litter and debris, and soil) serve various purposes in terms of measuring and monitoring an area’s carbon stock. For example, S-CAP can estimate carbon emissions from deforestation based on the change in aboveground biomass (AGB), which stores the majority of the carbon found within a tropical forest.

Area of Interest

The area of analysis for determining forest cover loss and biomass. This platform allows users to analyze data within a country, a protected area, or a user-defined region.

Land Use/Land Cover

Global

  • CCI: ESA's Climate Change initiative (CCI) land cover product is based on SAR imagery at 300m resolution and covers 2000-2020. Classes were reclassified to forest/nonforest.
  • ESRI: ESRI's 10m Sentinel-based land use and land cover map covering 2017-2021. The original 10-class product was reclassed to forest/nonforest.
  • JAXA: JAXA's 25m PALSAR-based forest map covering 2007-2020. The original 3-class product (2007-2017) and 4-class product (2017-2020) was reclassed to only forest/nonforest by masking out water. Gaps in the data are due to ALOS issues.
  • MODIS: MODIS MCD12Q1 500m land cover generated through combined imagery from Terra and Aqua satellites covers 2001-2020. The 17 classes of LC_Type1 was reclassfied to forest/non forest.
  • WorldCover: ESA Worldcover's 10m Sentinel-1 & Sentinel-2 based product provides global coverage for 2020 and 2021. The treecover classification was labeled as forest with all others as nonforest
  • GFW: Hansen's Global Forest Change dataset, generated using a time-series of Landsat imagery at 30m, was processed in Google Earth Engine to estimate annual tree cover from 2001-2020.

Regional

  • MAGA: Guatemala’s “Determination of Vegetated Cover and Land Use at scale 1:50,000 for the Republic of Guatemala” for year 2020 was created based on imagery from Sentinel-1A from 2020
  • MapBiomass: Collection-4 land cover maps for the Amazon Basin covering 2000-2021 at 30m Landsat resolution and reclassified to forest/nonforest
  • NLCMS: Nepal's National Land Cover Monitoring System, developed with support from SERVIR-HKH provides annual coverage of 2000-2019 at 30m resolution.
  • RCMRD: The Regional Centre for Mapping of Resources for Development (RCMRD) generated land use and land cover maps at the regional scale for Eastern and Southern Africa. These 30m resolution maps cover 2000-2017 and are reclassified to forest/nonforest.
  • RLCMS_HKH: The Regional Land Cover Monitoring System (RLCMS) for the Hindu Kush Himalaya (HKH) region provides annual land cover maps from 2000-2021 and are reclassified to forest/nonforest The Regional Land Cover Monitoring System (RLCMS) for the Hindu Kush Himalaya (HKH) region provides annual land cover maps from 2000-2021 and are reclassified to forest/nonforest
  • RLCMS_SEA: The Regional Land Cover Monitoring System (RLCMS) for the Southeast Asia (SEA) region provides annual land cover maps from 2000-2018 and are reclassified to forest/nonforest
  • USGS: A joint initiative with the US Geological Survey produced 3 land use land cover maps (1975, 2000, 2013) over West Africa at a 2-km resolution. This classification was reclassed to forest/nonforest.
